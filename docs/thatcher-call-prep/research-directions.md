# Research Directions for Thatcher Call Prep

Things to research before the discovery call. Assign to research Claude (Gemini/Clara).

---

## 1. Thatcher's specific regulatory exposure in California

Alexander runs the California plant. California has Cal/OSHA, which is a state-plan state with its own enforcement and often stricter requirements than federal OSHA. Research:
- Cal/OSHA PSM requirements vs federal OSHA PSM — any differences?
- California-specific chemical reporting requirements (Prop 65, CalARP)
- Any Cal/OSHA enforcement actions against Thatcher's California facilities specifically
- CalARP (California Accidental Release Prevention) program requirements — this is California's version of EPA RMP and may have additional training mandates

Why it matters: If you can reference a California-specific requirement that Alexander hasn't thought about, you demonstrate deep domain knowledge. "Are your current SOPs covering CalARP requirements too, or just federal RMP?" is a question that earns instant credibility with a California plant manager.

## 2. What Thatcher's competitors do for training

Research how comparable mid-size chemical manufacturers and distributors handle compliance training:
- Hydrite Chemical Co. (their JV partner) — any public info on their training approach?
- Brenntag, Univar, Hawkins — these are larger chemical distributors. Do they have in-house training programs? Have they published anything about training modernization?
- Any case studies from training vendors (Convergence Training, Vector Solutions, SafetySkills) featuring chemical manufacturers?

Why it matters: If you can say "companies your size in the chemical space typically..." you're framing the conversation around industry norms, not just your opinion.

## 3. OSHA inspection and violation database for Thatcher

You know about the 2019 willful violations from the research briefing, but dig deeper:
- Search OSHA's public enforcement database (osha.gov/ords/imis) for all Thatcher Company inspections
- What specific standards were cited? (29 CFR numbers)
- Which facilities were inspected?
- Were any citations specifically about inadequate training documentation?
- Any recent inspections (2024-2026) that might be driving the urgency?

Why it matters: You will NOT bring this up on the call. But if Alexander mentions audit pressure or "we've had some findings," you'll know exactly what he's referring to and can respond with genuine understanding rather than generic sympathy.

## 4. Chemical-specific training requirements for their products

Thatcher handles sodium hypochlorite (chlorine), ferric sulfate, sulfuric acid, hydrochloric acid, caustic soda, hydrogen peroxide, ammonium hydroxide, and more. Research:
- Which of these chemicals trigger PSM threshold quantities?
- Which require specific GHS/HazCom training elements?
- Are there industry-specific training standards beyond OSHA? (ACC Responsible Care, NACD Responsible Distribution)
- What does DOT hazmat training specifically require for drivers handling these chemicals?

Why it matters: When Alexander says "we handle chlorine," you want to know that sodium hypochlorite in bulk storage likely triggers PSM thresholds and requires specific emergency response training. That level of specificity is what separates you from a generic training vendor.

## 5. The "SOP digitization" landscape

Research what tools and vendors specifically market "SOP digitization" to chemical/manufacturing companies:
- Dozuki, MasterControl, Qualio, ComplianceQuest — what do they offer and what do they charge?
- Are any of these specifically targeting chemical manufacturers?
- What's the typical implementation timeline and cost for these platforms?
- How do they handle training vs. document management? (Most are document management, not training)

Why it matters: Alexander may have already looked at some of these. If he says "we looked at Dozuki" you want to know what that is and how you're different. The key distinction: these platforms digitize the DOCUMENT. You digitize the LEARNING. They make SOPs searchable. You make SOPs stick.

## 6. Thatcher's Sacramento/Stockton operations specifically

Dig into what happens at Alexander's plant:
- What products does the Sacramento facility manufacture? (The Sac-Ag JV with Hydrite produces thiosulfate chemistries)
- How big is the Sacramento facility? (Research mentions a 7,000 sq ft manufacturing/storage building with railroad spur)
- Are there any permits, environmental reports, or Tier II filings for the Sacramento facility that reveal what chemicals are on-site?
- Any local news about the facility?

Why it matters: The more you know about HIS plant specifically, the more the conversation feels like you've already started the work. "Your Sacramento facility handles thiosulfate production for the Sac-Ag venture — does that operation have its own SOPs separate from the main Thatcher procedures?" shows you've done your homework.

## 7. Training completion rates and engagement benchmarks

Find industry data on:
- Typical compliance training completion rates in chemical manufacturing
- Average time to complete mandatory training per employee per year
- Any studies comparing narrative/interactive training vs. traditional read-and-sign approaches in industrial settings
- Cost of non-compliance (OSHA penalty averages for training violations in chemical sector)

Why it matters: If you can say "the industry average for voluntary training completion is 60%, but companies using interactive modules see 85-95%" — that's a data point Alexander can put in his recommendation to Lauren Stein. You're giving him ammunition for the internal sell.
