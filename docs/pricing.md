# Pricing Guide

## Pricing Philosophy

Price from the buyer's alternative, not your cost. Their alternative is a $40K-$80K agency project that takes 3-4 months, or a $5/user/month checkbox tool nobody remembers. We sit in the gap between expensive custom and cheap generic — custom quality at a price mid-market companies can justify.

**The anchor for every conversation:**

> "Traditional training development runs $40,000 to $80,000 per course and takes 3-4 months. We deliver the same quality — with narrative scripting, 20+ custom illustrations per module, quizzes, and a full audit trail — in 2-3 weeks."

Say this before you say a number. Let them do the math. Whatever you quote next sounds reasonable against that anchor.

**Four principles:**
1. Price from the buyer's alternative, not your cost
2. Bigger company = bigger budget = bigger number (same work, more impact)
3. Never discount the first project — the demo module proves the value
4. The number should make you slightly uncomfortable. If it feels safe, it's too low.

## Target Market: Mid-Market (100-500 employees)

Multi-branch credit unions, regional senior living chains, mid-size manufacturers. Training budget $100K-$500K/year. Has a training coordinator or compliance team. Regulated industries that MUST train annually.

**Why mid-market is the sweet spot:**
- Large enough budgets to pay $15K-$25K per course
- Too small to have internal L&D teams that build everything in-house
- Regulated enough that training is non-discretionary
- Examiner scrutiny makes the audit trail a genuine differentiator
- The compliance officer is your champion — they need proof the training covers requirements

The product is a complete learning experience — a narrative arc across multiple modules that builds understanding progressively. Always quote the course first. Quote modules only if asked.

### What's Included in a Pilot Course

- Full SOP analysis and training brief
- Client Q&A cycle (gap identification, contradiction resolution)
- 5-6 narrative modules (15-20 minutes each) in SOPs house voice
- 20+ custom illustrations per module, consistent visual identity
- Interactive HTML player (self-paced, responsive, no LMS required)
- SCORM package (1.2 or 2004) if client has an LMS
- Quizzes with scoring and completion tracking
- Full audit report (traceability matrix, compliance coverage map, review history)
- One round of revision per module based on review feedback
- Client expertise file (accumulated knowledge of their domain)

### What's Included in an Annual Program

Everything in the pilot course, plus:
- 2-3 courses per year (10-18 modules total)
- Ongoing SOP monitoring (drift detection when source docs change)
- Annual module updates when regulations or procedures change
- Growing expertise file that makes each course faster and better
- Priority review scheduling
- Quarterly compliance coverage audit

## Assessment (entry point at every tier)

**$2,500 - $3,500**

For prospects who aren't ready to commit to a course:
- Full SOP analysis and training brief
- Knowledge gap identification and client questionnaire
- Recommended module structure with estimated timeline
- One demo module (abbreviated, 8-10 slides) showing the quality level
- If they proceed to a full course, the assessment fee applies as credit

**This is the discovery call deliverable.** The demo module sells the course.

## Module Pricing (available if asked)

For clients who already have courses and need additions, or who want to start smaller.

**Single module (new style):** Use the per-tier rates above. This is more expensive per-module than buying a course because style establishment, client onboarding, and expertise file creation all happen for just one module.

**Style-matched module:** Lower rate (see table above). Matches the visual identity of existing modules. Cheaper because the style manifest and expertise file already exist. This is the upsell — client buys a course, comes back for additions, and the new modules feel like a natural continuation at a lower price point.

**Module pricing goes down as the relationship matures.** The first module for a new client costs the most. The fifth module costs less. The expertise file, style manifest, and client relationship are all compounding assets. Make this visible to the client — it incentivizes ongoing engagement.

## Discounting Guidelines

### When to Discount

| Situation | Discount | Rationale |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 3+ courses at once (annual program) | 10-15% | Volume efficiency — expertise file makes each course faster |
| Referral from past client | 10% | Reward referrers |
| Non-profit/education | 15-20% | Goodwill, case study potential |
| Case study permission (named, public) | 10% | Marketing value — their name on your portfolio |

### When NOT to Discount

- **First project.** Ever. The demo module proves the value. If they need convincing, the assessment is the low-risk entry.
- Rush jobs (charge MORE, not less)
- Price shoppers comparing to DIY tools (wrong customer — let them go)
- Scope creep disguised as "just one more thing"
- Any customer who doesn't value the work

### Discount Framing

**Never say:** "I'll give you a discount"

**Instead say:**
- "Since you're committing to the annual program, I can offer $X for the full engagement"
- "As a thank you for [referrer]'s referral, I can offer $X"
- "If we can feature this as a named case study, I can bring the program to $X"

Make it a specific earned circumstance, not your normal price.

## Objection Handling

**"That's expensive for training"**
> "Traditional training companies charge $40-80K for this scope and take 3-4 months. Off-the-shelf compliance courses run $5/user but they're generic — your examiner can tell, and your employees click through without learning anything. We're the middle ground: custom quality, fast delivery, with documentation that satisfies regulators."

**"I can use AI to do this myself"**
> "You can, and some of my clients tried. They discovered that getting AI to produce *good* training — narrative-driven, properly scoped to your procedures, with quiz questions that test real competence — takes 40-60 hours of prompt engineering, instructional design, image generation, and SCORM packaging. Most decided their time was worth more. But if you want to try it, I'd genuinely recommend it — you'll learn a lot about what your training needs, and I'm here if you decide you'd rather hand it off."

**"Can you do it for less?"**
> "What's your budget? Let me see if we can adjust scope to fit. We could start with the assessment — that gives you one demo module and a full training brief, and if you proceed to the full course, the assessment fee applies as credit."

**"We already have a training vendor"**
> "Great — have them build a module the way they normally do, and I'll build one from the same source document. Show both to your team and see which one they'd actually want to take. If theirs is better, you've lost nothing. If mine is, let's talk."

**"I need to think about it"**
> "Of course. The assessment is the low-risk way to evaluate — you get a demo module built from your actual documents, so you can see exactly what the full course would look like. This quote is good for 30 days."

## Quoting Complex Projects

### Example: Mid-Market Credit Union BSA/AML Program

**Client:** Regional credit union, 200 employees across 8 branches
**Need:** Annual BSA/AML compliance training + new-employee onboarding

| Component | Modules | Price |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| BSA/AML Fundamentals (all staff) | 5 | $18,000 |
| Advanced BSA (compliance team) | 3 | $9,000 |
| New Employee Onboarding | 4 | $10,000 |
| **Year 1 Total (12 modules)** | | **$37,000** |
| Annual update retainer (Year 2+) | | $6,000-$9,600/yr |

*At $37K for Year 1, this is still less than half what an agency would charge for comparable scope. The annual retainer gives them updated modules when regulations change, without paying for full redevelopment.*

### How to Scope

1. Get all source documents first
2. Count distinct training topics (each becomes a module or part of one)
3. Estimate total content (1 page of SOP ≈ 2-3 min of training)
4. Group into courses of 4-6 modules each (natural learning arcs)
5. Quote the course(s), not the modules
6. Add annual retainer for ongoing updates
7. Pad 10% for unknowns

## Notes

- The assessment is always the entry point. Never skip it, even for eager buyers. The client Q&A cycle surfaces gaps that improve the final product.
- Style-matched modules are the retention engine. First course establishes the visual identity; subsequent modules extend it at lower cost. Make this explicit to the client.
- The audit trail is a differentiator, not an add-on. Include it in every engagement. Compliance officers sell the next engagement internally using the audit report.
- Annual programs are the goal. Course → retainer → annual program. The expertise file compounds and the effective rate climbs.
