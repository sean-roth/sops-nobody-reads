**Status:** Scripts complete, ready for LXD decomposition

## The Thesis

The procedure is simple. Six steps. A padlock. A tag. That simplicity is exactly what makes it dangerous — because simple feels like something you can shortcut.

The gap isn't knowledge. Everyone who's been through LOTO training can recite the steps. The gap is **respect for the simple thing.** The moment you treat a six-step procedure like it's beneath your attention is the moment it fails to protect you.

**Core argument:** This simple thing is the only thing between you and the energy that will kill you. So let's make sure you actually do it right every time, not just the times you're thinking about it.

## Visual Direction: Sequential Art

**Inspiration:** Scott McCloud — sequential art principles applied to industrial safety training.

**Theme:** Industrial (new fourth theme)
- Dark backgrounds with concrete/metal texture
- High-visibility accent colors (safety yellow/orange)
- Monospace for procedures, humanist sans for narration
- Panel-based slide layouts (gutters between content blocks)

**Visual Vocabulary:**
- Human presence without identity (hands, silhouettes, no faces)
- The camera is the coworker (10 feet away, floor level)
- Equipment as character (the physical world IS the visual)
- Typography carries the heaviest moments
- The implication gutter handles consequences we never depict
